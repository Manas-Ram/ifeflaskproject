Retirement

Invest now for the life you want in the future

  1. Why should you invest for retirement?
    It reduces the amount of taxes you owe on the income for each year you invest in it.
    It allows you to defer or even avoid the taxes you owe on the earnings that you get for investing.
    It produces earnings on earnings, so you can make money anywhere anytime which is not
    available in a regular savings account.

  2. What is an IRA and should I use it?
    Traditional IRA's (Individual Retirement Account) allow you to place in pre-tax money into retirement.
    Many financial experts estimate that you may need up to 85% of your pre-retirement income in retirement.

  3. Which IRA should I use?
    IRA's are designed differently with different advantages. It is whatever is catered to you.
    - In a traditional IRA, you make contributions with money you may be able to deduct on your tax return. Earnings potentially tax-deferred when you withdraw. In a Roth IRA, You make contributions with money you've already paid taxes on (after-tax), money can potentially grow tax-free, with tax-free withdrawals in retirement. A Rollover IRA takes money from another retirement plan and transfers it. You contribute money "rolled over" from a qualified retirement plan into this traditional IRA. Rollovers involve moving eligible assets from an employer-sponsored plan, such as a 401(k) or 403(b), into an IRA
